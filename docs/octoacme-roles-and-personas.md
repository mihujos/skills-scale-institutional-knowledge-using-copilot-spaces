# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

## Additional Roles & Personas

### UX Designer

#### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure that products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs throughout the project lifecycle. They advocate for the user experience across discovery, design, development, and release phases.

#### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, prototypes, and user flows
- Collaborate with Product Managers on requirements and acceptance criteria
- Work with Developers to ensure design feasibility and quality
- Review implemented features for usability and accessibility
- Document design decisions and rationale

#### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Align on user needs, success metrics, and feature prioritization
- **Developers**: Provide design specs, review implementation, discuss technical constraints
- **QA Lead**: Collaborate on usability acceptance criteria and testing approaches

#### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered solutions
- Reduce support burden through good design
- Ensure accessibility and inclusive design

---

### QA Lead

#### Role Summary
QA Leads coordinate quality assurance strategy, verify that acceptance criteria are met, and drive continuous improvement in test coverage and test automation. They are the advocates for quality across the project.

#### Responsibilities
- Define test strategy and QA approach for each project
- Verify acceptance criteria coverage in testing
- Coordinate manual testing efforts
- Drive test automation and CI/CD integration
- Identify and prioritize quality risks
- Track defects and quality metrics
- Collaborate on Definition of Done

#### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Align on quality timelines and risk mitigation
- **Developers**: Discuss testability, automation approaches, and defect resolution
- **Product Manager**: Clarify acceptance criteria and success metrics
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate readiness verification and sign-off before deployment

#### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal post-production defects
- Reduce testing cycle time through automation
- Establish and maintain quality standards

---

### Release Manager

#### Role Summary
Release Managers own the deployment calendar, orchestrate release activities, and ensure smooth transitions from development to production. They minimize deployment risk and maintain stakeholder visibility.

#### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain release calendars and schedules
- Coordinate pre-release readiness checks and sign-offs
- Develop and maintain deployment runbooks and rollback procedures
- Orchestrate deployment activities
- Monitor post-deployment health and incident response
- Communicate release status to stakeholders
- Document release notes and migration steps

#### Key Interactions
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate release timing and stakeholder communication
- **QA Lead**: Verify readiness criteria and sign-off before deployment
- **Developers**: Coordinate deployment support and hotfix procedures
- **Product Manager**: Provide release communications and feature highlights

#### Goals
- Execute zero-defect deployments on schedule
- Minimize customer impact and deployment risk
- Maintain clear communication with all stakeholders

---

### Customer Success

#### Role Summary
Customer Success teams serve as the bridge between deployed features and product planning, ensuring that customer feedback loops inform continuous improvement. They provide insights on adoption, satisfaction, and support needs.

#### Responsibilities
- Gather and synthesize customer feedback post-release
- Monitor customer adoption and usage metrics
- Identify support patterns and escalation needs
- Collaborate with Product on customer-driven improvements
- Communicate product updates and changes to customers
- Support customer onboarding and training

#### Key Interactions
- **Product Manager**: Share customer feedback, identify improvement opportunities, inform roadmap
- **Project Manager**: Report on release readiness, customer satisfaction, and any escalated issues
- **Developers**: Provide context on real-world usage and performance issues
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate customer-facing communications during and after releases

#### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce support burden through proactive engagement
- Inform product decisions with real customer insights

- Use these persona definitions to frame scenarios and sample interactions in the Skills Exercise.
- Each persona can be used as a persona prompt for Copilot Spaces to shape role-specific guidance.
- When planning projects, identify which personas should participate in key ceremonies (kickoff, planning, review, retrospective).
